Why are there no Indian's in Congress?
We can change this.

Dear member of the United States India Political Action committee,

People from India have done well in America. We have two governors, Nikki Haley and Bobby Jindal, and many successful scientists and businessmen and people who have distinguished themselves in other important ways.

Why are there no Indian American's in Congress, and how can we change this? I think Statesmen PAC has an answer.

"It is my goal to get 10 Indian Americans elected to Congress."

If we are to get Indian's elected to Congress we need a way for them to have a voice and stature within a meaningful political community.

We need political tools and talent to mentor new candidates and manage campaigns so they can win.

Statesmen PAC is a new interface between you and congressional candidates. It's a place where you can get connected with a million people interested in better government, experts in various fields, including you! This is where we will have conversations about vital issues and find common ground. This is how we reach a consensus and produce resolution on important issues.We have the way to deliver our consent to our representatives, our congressmen.

This is your direct voice.

I hope you will consider giving $199, the maximum this "MicroPAC" will accept and have other family members and friends do likewise. By contributing you join the PAC and you can help identify Indian American and other statesmen around the country who we all can support and help elect. StatesmenPAC will provide a network to clarify issues and the tools to help statesmen-like candidates win. It is my goal to get 10 Indian Americans elected to Congress. This is a big goal, but it can be accomplished by funding StatesmenPAC, identifying statesmen among us and supporting them with our contributions and helping them build grassroots organizations in their districts.

Can you imagine the effect of 100 extraordinary men and women, people significantly beyond the stature of our current congressmen, running together and being asked why they are so different from common candidates? And can you imagine 10 Indian Americans among them? I can. Please help me make this happen by supporting StatesmenPAC, a MicroPAC, with a $199 contribution from yourself, your family members and your friends.

Thanks for your support of our community,


Sanjay Puri
