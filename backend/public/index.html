A Statesman

is a person who puts the interests of the country above his or her own narrow interests and who has distinguished himself in some important way, be it career or community. One way to get an idea about candidates, or serving congressmen, is to ask a couple of questions:

  • Would they be making more money in public service than they have earned before?
  • If they had distinguished themselves in a service area, did they really help or were they just climbing an organization to get a better paying job?

We expect

Statesmen to have clear ideas about policy. Political affiliation isn't as important as being willing to discuss and compromise to actually improve America. Statesmen put policy ahead of politics. We expect them to agree with:

  • A willingness to limit the size of contributions they accept. (StatesmenPAC is a MicroPAC, after all)
  • A process of crowdsourcing policy so legislation is transparent and acceptable to their constituents.

Political Tools

We believe, along with Sean Parker, who is developing brigade.com, that the next internet revolution is going to be politics. We believe we will get better representatives and better law if citizens use online tools to coordinate their political activity. And we think crowdsourcing solutions will actually improve America quickly.

We are indifferent to sourcing tools. We expect that Brigade.com’s products will be spectacular. We are working with a couple of groups to provide improvements in what is currently available. We will list tools soon that can be used today for social organization

Statesmen: Advisory Board

Chris Cannon

U.S. House of Representatives (1997-2009)

Advisory Board Purpose:
The advisory board will be comprised of people of clear, explicit political vision, paired generally as lefties and righties, who love America. This means that they love the institutions (like our banking system or the Uniform Commercial Code or religions competing in an environment of free speech), our foundation in principles embodied in the Constitution which, in turn, are built on the explicit principles of the Declaration of Independence. We recognize the responsibility that governing in a changing world sometimes requires new laws or institutions and we are committed to finding solutions to our problems which are consistent with the concepts that have made America great.
Advisory Board Agreement:
If an advisory board member ever decides to leave the board, Statesmen PAC will post his reasons for at least five days on the front page of the StatesmenPAC website.
